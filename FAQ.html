Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Space Simulation Center?

The Space Simulation Center, or the Space Center as it is commonly called, is any one of futuristic spaceship flight simulators created by various companies. These simulators, built in real life, allow you to take on the roles of a starship crew, and fly out to negotiate with aliens and battle with pirates.

How did it all start? (Who started it?)

The Space Center opened in 1990 at Central Elementary School in Pleasant Grove, Utah. It's creator and director, Victor Williamson, had thought of the idea while taking his Young Astronaut Club students to a conference in Japan. Through several years of effort, writing letters for grants, and receiving permission from School Administrators, among other things, an addition to the school, where Victor was teaching, was built, and the Space Center began.

What is the Space Center's purpose? / Why is it here?

The Space Center originally opened with the intent of educating students in STEM concepts, but has developed from purely education to entertainment flight simulations as well.

How does it keep going?

The Space Center has touched the lives of many people, and often time changes their life. It continues to thrive by those people who are dedicated to spreading this experience throughout the world.

How can I get involved?

There are a variety of ways to get involved at a space center near you:

  • Volunteer at a center near you
  • Creating Content
  • Getting Hired
  • Programming
  • Spreading the word
  • Start a Young Astronaut Club at your school.
  • Donate to your local space center. Contact the director of the center you want to donate to for more information.
  • Book a private mission.

Where is the Space Center Located?

The majority of space centers are located in Utah County, Utah. However, there are a few in Pennsylvania, and even Venezuela!

When will it come near me?

InfiniD Learning is striving to bring a space simulator to a school near you, however no official plans have been announced for where or when space centers will be built.

Can I make a Space Center?

Absolutely! You can go it on your own using the DIY Instrutions, or get in contact with either InfiniD Learning, Dreamflight, or Telos

What can the a Space Center do for me / my child / my business / my school?

Each space center is unique in the programs and classes that each offer. Some offer stellar private and corporate team-building flights, others over excellent classes teaching how to act, engineer, design, and write.
