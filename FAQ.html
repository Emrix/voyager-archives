Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Space Simulation Center?

The Space Simulation Center, or the Space Center as it is commonly called, is any one of futuristic spaceship flight simulators created by various companies. These simulators, built in real life, allow you to take on the roles of a starship crew, and fly out to negotiate with aliens and battle with pirates.

How can I get involved?

There are a variety of ways to get involved at a space center near you:

  • Volunteering

    Try

  • Creating Content
  • Getting Hired
  • Programming
  • Spreading the word
  • Start a Young Astronaut or Voyager Club at your school. The Voyager is here to serve as your club's simulator. Visit YoungAstronautsClub.org for more information on the program for grades 3 through 6. Visit VoyagerClub.org for information on the program for grades 7 - 12. I'll be happy to get your started. You can contact me at director@spacecamputah.org.
  • Volunteer to work with us at Farpoint by helping with the after school math tutoring program, the Young Astronauts and Voyager space education program, the Long Duration Mission program, and the GoogleCS Programming Classes.
  • Donate to space education by giving generously to Farpoint. Your donation is tax deductible. Go to Renacademy.org; then "Online Payments". Look for the "Farpoint" link.
  • Book the USS Voyager for a private mission. Money spent on a Voyager mission goes into the ship's upkeep. You can book a Voyager mission by visiting DiscoverySpaceCenter.org. then go to private missions. You may also book a private mission through our web site, spacecamputah.org.
